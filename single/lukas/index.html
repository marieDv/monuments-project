back A video-walk through the Lüderitzstrasse in Berlin lets you explore the hidden colonial context of the street. By using simple means of juxtaposition and embodied movement, it creates an augmented environment that allows the viewer to shift perspective and experience a deeper emotional understanding of the ongoing discourse.

Here memories, emotions, cultural understanding, political and ethical world views and a sense of socio-economic value, collide with each other to create a conflict zone that can’t be solved through arguments and good reasoning alone. The feeling of never quite being able to grasp the whole picture of this conflict is a struggle rooted in the complexity of its historical background and the otherness of its oppositional parties. Through exploring the many layers and narratives contained in this place, I hope to open up the viewer’s perspective towards the problematics of colonial legacies in public space, the topic of decolonialization in general and the critical dialog surrounding the commemorative culture in Germany.
What is missing here is a connecting link. A format that allows for the fusion of the physical shape and the historical and emotional heritage – building a bridge between past and present, between Germany and Namibia and between the many voices of this conflict. This video is an attempt to create such a space.

Through exploring the many layers and narratives contained in this place, I hope to open up the viewer’s perspective towards the problematics of colonial legacies in public space and in our minds, the topic of decolonialization in general and the critical dialog surrounding the commemorative culture in Germany. Adolf Lüderitz, tobacco merchant and founder of the colony German South West Africa.
