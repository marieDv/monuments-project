The Memorial on the Dr Karl Lueger platz *
Dr. Karl Lueger died on March 10, 1910. Just one day later, the first donations for the erection of a Lueger memorial were received by the Reichspost. Two weeks after his death, the Christian Social Party applied to the city council for the erection of a Lueger monument on the square in front of the New Town Hall. Contemporaries criticized that the memorial was not only supposed to be a place for rememberance but also a display of power of the political party whose representative Lueger had been considered: “…. The significance of passed events should be demonstrated to the whole world, but especially to the parties opponents ... " One criticized - to put it casually - the megalomania of the monument: the huge sum of money that was made available & the space where it was supposed to be set up (Rathausplatz) The erection of the memorial was approved, but the question of the location should still lead to heated discussions - ultimately the memorial should be erected at the Dr. Karl-Lueger-Platz, that is located on the Stubenring.
Initially the memorial was supposed to be placed in front of the Rathaus - one of the most prominent spots in the city.

The father of right wing populism in current disussions
During Dr.Karl Luegers tenure, Vienna gained the negative reputation of the first anti-Semitically governed metropolis in Europe. Lueger realized the effectiveness of a demagogically and rhetorically employed "hostility to Jews" and used it for his rise to power in Vienna. Lueger founded and lead the Christian Social party in the 1880s, which quickly rivaled the Social Democrats (Sozialdemokratische Partei Österreichs, SPÖ) that had some famous jewish members such as Otto Bauer. Anti-Semitism united the Christian Social Party and contributed to bringing Lueger into the position of major of Vienna which he should inherit from 1897 to 1910. Luegers anti-Semitic political views are often described as purely opportunistic. He took advantage of the economically grounded anti-Jewish mood in which Jewish industrialists and bankers were considered to be the cause of social problems. Hitler admired Lueger for his speech-making skills and effective use of propaganda in gaining popular appeal. He studied Lueger carefully and modeled some of his later behavior on what he learned.

After his death in 1910 the Christian Social Party continued to create illustrations and posters of him that depicted his fight against the Jewish “Mammon” and Liberalism. some politically motivated groups still symbolize and idealize Lueger and use his memorial as a gathering point for speeches and demonstrations that often follow an anti-immigrant and xenophobic agenda.

Transforming Luegers Legacy
It is hard to tell how much of the admiration towards Lueger is based on his achievements as a major and what’s the remains of an intense personal cult and the instrumentalization of his character to achieve political goals during his lifetime and after. It can be said with certainty that his contemporary popularity has little to do with his actual achievements and more so with the use of his figure from various political groups that kept his memory alive. A statue that is used as gathering point for right-wing fascists that keeps this legacy of anti-Semitic and Xenophobia alive cannot stay untouched. Multiple remainescenants of Luegers time as a major have already been removed. The Green party already pushed for the renaming of the “Dr Karl Lueger Ring”, one of the most central roads in Vienna in 2012. There were also plans to contextualize the memorial of Dr Karl Lueger further, that were not approved by their coalition partner the Social Democratic Party of Austria.

2016 Vienna's City Councilor for Culture Andreas Mailath-Pokorny and District Chairman Markus Figl unveiled a table for the Lueger monument on Dr.-Karl-Lueger-Platz. The text is written by historian Oliver Rathkolb in collaboration with the Vienna 1 Cultural Commission.

“The table reminds of both sides of Karl Lueger, of the energetic mayor who drove the city's development into a modern metropolis, as well as of the politician who made use of political anti-Semitism and reinforced nationalistic tendencies of his time. The monument can only be understood from its time and is placed in a historical context through the information on the board. The Wienkl complements the Lueger monument in a clear, informative way. This will continue the efforts of the City of Vienna to address history and raise awareness, ”says Vienna's City Councilor for Culture Andreas

Following the Black Lives Matter movement that gained momentum during this year Austrian activists sprayed the word “Schande” (shame) over the monument to call out for its removal or a proper contextualization. This was also a part of the election programs during the Viennese election 2020. All candidates gave statements on the issue and rated the topic on a scale on relevancy.
